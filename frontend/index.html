Aircraft

Study hub for the training aircraft, G1000 avionics, procedures and pre-flight preparation.

C172 Diesel

Open C172 Diesel POH

The POH is the reference document. Use this section as a teaching guide before aircraft handling and cockpit preparation.

  • Know aircraft limitations, normal procedures and emergency procedures from the POH.
  • Verify fuel type, fuel quantity, technical status and required documents before flight.
  • Monitor engine indications throughout the flight, not only during run-up.
  • Use checklist discipline: flow first, then checklist verification where appropriate.

Garmin G1000

Understand the PFD/MFD split and never allow the avionics to replace basic flying priorities: attitude, power, trim, lookout.

  • PFD: attitude, IAS, altitude, VSI, HSI/CDI, bugs and alerts.
  • MFD: moving map, engine indications, flight plan, nearest airports and system pages.
  • Always confirm CDI source: GPS / VOR / LOC.
  • Verbalize active leg and nav source before departure and before approach.

Pre-flight preparation

  • Weather, NOTAMs and route threats.

CPL Modular

Commercial pilot training, stage by stage.

The modular route is designed for flexibility: the student can complete the training in modules, keep breaks between stages, and build the program around personal schedule, budget, work, and life constraints.

✈
Breaks between modulesTrain at a pace that fits life and budget.
Skip completed modulesCredit previous valid training where accepted by the ATO.
More flexibilityPlan hour building and CPL preparation around your availability.

Why choose CPL Modular?

Similar concept to the BAA modular explanation: flexible, staged training instead of one continuous integrated course.

Flexible route

Useful for students who cannot stop life completely for a full-time integrated program. You can plan the next phase when ready.

Tailor-made planning

Training can be arranged around Israeli travel windows, work obligations, family constraints, weather, aircraft availability and learning pace.

Professional standard

The final goal is the same: commercial-standard flying, disciplined preparation, accurate handling and readiness for the CPL skill test.

01
Before training

Assessment & personal plan

Review licence status, medical, logbook, English level, previous experience, target timeline and student constraints. The output is a personal training plan, not a copy-paste schedule.

02
Foundation

PPL(A) / Existing licence check

Students doing PPL(A) begin with core handling, circuits, solo, navigation and skill test preparation. Students who already hold PPL continue after verifying validity and readiness.

03
Experience

Hour building

Structured PIC experience, navigation confidence, weather decisions, airspace awareness, diversion practice and professional habits. This is where the student matures from “licence holder” to “commercial candidate”.

04
Theory

CPL / ATPL theoretical knowledge

Complete the required theoretical knowledge route according to the licence goal and authority/ATO plan. Theory progress must support the practical phase, not run disconnected from it.

05
CPL phase

CPL(A) modular flight training

Training focuses on commercial-level precision, decision making, abnormal handling, navigation, diversion, performance, TEM and cockpit discipline. The ATO decides the exact syllabus and readiness gates.

06
Final gate

Mock test & CPL skill test

Final consolidation includes profile practice, oral preparation, aircraft documents, weather, performance, mass and balance, emergency handling and examiner-standard decision making.

Typical requirement checklist

This is a planning checklist for your website. The legal source of truth remains the current EASA Part-FCL and the approved ATO training manual.

MedicalEASA Class 1 medical should be in place before CPL issue and ideally before investing heavily in commercial training.
AgePlan toward the CPL issue age requirement. The ATO should verify before skill-test planning.
Licence / experiencePPL(A), required experience, PIC time and navigation experience must be checked against the approved modular pathway.
TheoryCPL/ATPL theoretical knowledge route according to the student’s target and authority requirements.
ReadinessATO progress checks, instructor recommendation and all documents complete before skill test.
