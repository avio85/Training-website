Aircraft

C152 / C172 Diesel / Garmin G1000 student preparation module.

Study before first flight

Know what you are looking at before the engine starts.

This module teaches the aircraft, cockpit, G1000 scan, flight data and preparation habits. Videos are external references; actual procedures must follow your aircraft POH, checklist and ATO procedures.

PFD
MFD
ENG

C172 Diesel aircraft

Focus areas: diesel engine management, electrical system, fuel checks, engine indications, limitations, abnormal indications and normal checklist discipline.

  • Understand what the engine instruments are telling you.
  • Know the difference between checklist reading and flow discipline.
  • Build habit patterns for fuel, electrical and annunciator checks.

Garmin G1000 cockpit

The G1000 is not just a screen; it is the main aircraft information system. Students must understand PFD/MFD roles, CDI source, active leg, map range and engine page.

  • PFD: attitude, IAS, ALT, VSI, HSI, CDI, bugs.
  • MFD: map, flight plan, engine data, nearest airports.
  • Always verify selected NAV source before navigation tasks.

Instrument scan

Good scan prevents fixation. Primary flight data comes first, navigation second, automation last.

  • Look outside in VFR; use instruments to confirm.
  • Keep airspeed, attitude and altitude stable before programming.
  • Do not let the G1000 become the flight instructor.

Flight data review

Post-flight data helps debrief accurately: route tracking, altitude keeping, speed control, circuit spacing and lesson objectives.

  • Use data to improve next lesson planning.
  • Identify trends, not isolated small mistakes.
  • Connect data review to student self-assessment.

Modular CPL(A) Roadmap

A scrolling roadmap style: flexible, stage-by-stage training with pauses between modules when needed.

1
Start gate

Entry & personal plan

Review licence history, medical, logbook, budget, availability, work/family constraints and target timeline. The training plan is built around the student’s real schedule.

2
Foundation

PPL(A)

For PPL students: complete core handling, circuits, solo, navigation and skill-test preparation. For students who already hold PPL, verify standards before moving forward.

3
Experience

Hour building

Structured PIC flights, cross-country planning, weather judgement, radio confidence and real operational discipline. Not just “collecting hours”.

4
Theory

ATPL / CPL theoretical knowledge

Complete the required theoretical knowledge path according to the student’s target authority and licence plan. Progress should support, not block, the flight schedule.

5
Prerequisites

Medical, language, night / additional elements

Confirm Class 1 medical planning, language requirements, night qualification and any required experience gates before CPL training starts.

6
Commercial standard

CPL modular flight training

Commercial handling accuracy, navigation discipline, diversion, emergency handling, performance, TEM and professional cockpit behaviour.

7
Readiness

Mock test & weak-area recovery

Mock profile, oral preparation, emergency decision making and focused correction of weak areas before examiner recommendation.

8
Final

CPL(A) skill test

Final examiner assessment when ATO confirms all prerequisites, training records and standardization requirements are complete.

Important

This is a planning roadmap, not a legal checklist. Exact hour requirements and prerequisites must be verified against current EASA Part-FCL, the ATO training manual and the student’s previous experience.
