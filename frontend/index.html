Aircraft & G1000 Study

Guest-accessible teaching pages for student preparation.

Page 1 — C172 Diesel Overview

Engine concept, fuel system, limitations, normal checks and engine management mindset.

  • Monitor power, temperature and annunciations.
  • Before start: fuel, electrical system and checklist discipline.
  • In flight: trends, abnormal indications and decisions.

Page 2 — Garmin G1000 Basics

PFD/MFD roles, HSI, CDI source, engine strip, flight plan and map awareness.

  • PFD: attitude, airspeed, altitude, HSI.
  • MFD: map, engine data, nearest airports.
  • Verbalize CDI source and active leg.

Page 3 — Instrument Scan & Flight Data

Read flight data, identify trends and use post-flight review.

  • Route vs actual track.
  • Altitude discipline and airborne time.
  • Use data to brief the next exercise.

Page 4 — C152 / Basic Trainer

Simple VFR trainer preparation: controls, fuel, performance, handling and energy.

  • Weight and balance mindset.
  • Energy management in circuit.
  • Checklist flow and callouts.

Modular CPL(A) Roadmap

Full roadmap and requirement overview for modular professional training.

From PPL to CPL Skill Test

Build the licence step by step — with gates before moving forward.

Training-planning overview. Exact requirements must be checked against the ATO training manual and current EASA Part-FCL.

PPLHoursTheoryCPL
01

Entry & Document Check

Identity, logbook, medical, English level, previous training records and ATO enrolment.

02

PPL(A)

Foundation flying, solo, navigation and PPL skill test. Some students may already hold this.

03

Hour Building

Structured PIC experience, cross-country planning, radio confidence and weather decisions.

04

Night / Add-ons

Complete required ratings or qualifications according to the pathway and ATO plan.

05

Theory

Theoretical knowledge to CPL/ATPL level depending on the student target path.

06

CPL Modular Training

Visual and instrument flying training in one continuous ATO course.

07

Skill Test Prep

Mock profiles, emergencies, navigation, diversion, performance and oral prep.

08

CPL(A) Skill Test

Final examiner assessment after ATO readiness confirmation.

Typical Entry Requirements

  • Age planning toward CPL privileges
  • Valid EASA Class 1 Medical before CPL issue
  • PPL(A) or equivalent stage
  • English and theory preparation

Experience Gates

  • PIC hour-building progression
  • Cross-country experience
  • Night / instrument elements as applicable
  • ATO readiness check

Commercial Standard

  • Accuracy and professional discipline
  • Threat and error management
  • Checklist discipline
  • Decision making under workload
